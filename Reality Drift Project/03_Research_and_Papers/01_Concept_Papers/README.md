# Reality Drift: Concept Papers

This collection defines the core structural mechanisms and conditions that underpin the Reality Drift framework.

Each document isolates a specific concept, ranging from foundational system dynamics to higher-level conditions that emerge at scale. Together, these papers form the conceptual backbone of how systems maintain coherence while losing alignment with reality.

---

## Scope

The concept papers in this directory address topics including:

- Filter fatigue and the cost of continuous curation  
- Optimization traps and hidden systemic erosion  
- Co-cognition, synthetic flow, and emergent cognitive stratification  
- Reality Drift as a diagnosable structural pattern  
- Synthetic realness and the collapse of authentic and artificial boundaries  
- Authenticity drift and performativity  
- Temporal drift and the loss of shared time  
- Cognitive compression, degradation, and burnout  
- Cognitive hygiene, constraints, and loop closure  

Each paper isolates a single concept and develops it as a standalone component of the broader framework, without reliance on narrative continuity or external commentary.

## Framework Context

These concept papers describe the underlying dynamics that drive Reality Drift:

- Systems operating on representations rather than direct reality  
- Internal coherence increasing while external alignment weakens  
- Feedback loops degrading or becoming symbolic  
- Compression enabling scale while introducing distortion  
- Cognition and culture becoming increasingly self-referential  

Reality Drift describes this broader pattern: systems continue to function, but gradually lose alignment with the reality they are meant to represent or respond to.
