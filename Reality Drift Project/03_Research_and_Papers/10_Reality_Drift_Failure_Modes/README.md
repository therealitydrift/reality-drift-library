# Reality Drift Failure Modes

A collection of briefing papers examining recurring failure modes that emerge as AI systems become increasingly dependent on representations, optimization, and abstraction.

These papers explore the structural mechanisms through which systems can remain coherent, functional, and seemingly successful while gradually losing alignment with meaning, reality, and corrective feedback.

The series extends the broader Reality Drift framework into AI alignment, cognition, and system-level failure analysis.

## Papers

## 01. Semantic Decoupling

**AI Alignment: The Problem Is Not Behavior, It Is Meaning**

[reality-drift_semantic-decoupling_alignment-fails-at-meaning.pdf](reality-drift_semantic-decoupling_alignment-fails-at-meaning.pdf)

Examines alignment as a problem of semantic fidelity rather than behavior, arguing that systems fail when the meaning behind instructions degrades across layers of representation and optimization.

## 02. Externalized Recursion Instability

**The Medium Is Changing the Mind: AI and the Restructuring of Cognition**

[reality-drift_externalized-recursion_cognition-loops-unstable.pdf](reality-drift_externalized-recursion_cognition-loops-unstable.pdf)

Explores how AI-mediated cognition creates recursive feedback loops between human thought and machine-generated representations, reshaping how cognition develops and stabilizes.

## 03. Loss of Correction Binding

**Constraint Collapse: Why Systems Stop Correcting Themselves**

[reality-drift_constraint-collapse_loss-of-correction-binding.pdf](reality-drift_constraint-collapse_loss-of-correction-binding.pdf)

Describes how systems lose the ability to self-correct when constraints weaken, allowing drift to accumulate while functionality and performance metrics remain intact.
