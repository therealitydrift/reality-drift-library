## The Basic Pattern

George Lakoff and Mark Johnson transformed how many scholars understand language, cognition, and meaning.

Their central argument was deceptively simple:

> Human beings do not merely use metaphors.
> 
> Human beings think through metaphors.

Metaphors are not decorative features of language.

They are cognitive structures.

They help organize perception, interpretation, reasoning, and action.

People understand abstract concepts through symbolic frameworks that shape how reality is experienced.

In many ways, Lakoff and Johnson revealed that meaning is structured long before conscious reasoning begins.

---

## What Lakoff and Johnson Saw

Lakoff and Johnson observed that everyday thinking relies upon conceptual metaphors.

Examples include:

- Argument is war.

- Time is money.

- Life is a journey.

- Knowledge is seeing.

- Ideas are objects.

These metaphors influence how people interpret experience.

They shape attention.

They guide reasoning.

They determine what becomes visible and what remains hidden.

The metaphor often disappears from awareness.

Yet its influence remains active.

Their work emphasized:

- conceptual metaphors

- embodied cognition

- meaning

- language

- symbolic structures

- cognitive framing

In many ways, they demonstrated that representations shape cognition long before individuals consciously evaluate them.

---

## Conceptual Metaphors

For Lakoff and Johnson, abstract understanding depends upon representational structures.

People do not encounter reality directly.

They interpret reality through frameworks that organize experience.

These frameworks simplify complexity.

They make thought possible.

They allow individuals to navigate environments too complicated to fully comprehend.

The process is necessary.

The challenge emerges when the framework becomes invisible.

The metaphor survives.

Awareness of the metaphor disappears.

The representation becomes mistaken for reality itself.

---

## What Lakoff and Johnson Did Not Yet See

Much of their work emerged before the rise of contemporary algorithmic systems.

They never fully encountered:

- social media platforms

- recommendation systems

- engagement optimization

- algorithmic feeds

- AI-generated content

- digital identity systems

- platform-mediated cognition

- large-scale representational environments

The metaphors they studied primarily existed within language.

Modern systems increasingly operate through representations that extend far beyond language.

This distinction matters.

The question is no longer simply:

> How do metaphors shape thought?

The question increasingly becomes:

> What happens when entire informational environments function as invisible cognitive frameworks?

---

## From Conceptual Metaphors to Representational Environments

The Reality Drift framework extends Lakoff and Johnson's insights into contemporary informational systems.

Modern societies increasingly rely on representations that function similarly to conceptual metaphors.

Examples include:

- KPI as performance

- dashboard as organization

- credential as capability

- follower count as influence

- engagement as value

- benchmark as competence

These representations begin as useful simplifications.

Over time, they become cognitive substitutes.

The representation becomes the thing people respond to.

The distinction remains.

Awareness of the distinction weakens.

The representation increasingly structures perception itself.

---

## The Drift Principle

Within the Reality Drift framework, this process is described through the Drift Principle:

> When representational systems increasingly shape perception, individuals and institutions gradually lose awareness of the distinction between representation and reality.

The metric survives.

The dashboard survives.

The profile survives.

The benchmark survives.

The representation remains visible.

The reality becomes harder to observe directly.

Lakoff and Johnson explained how conceptual metaphors shape cognition.

Reality Drift examines what happens when entire informational environments operate as cognitive frameworks.

## Modern Examples

Many contemporary phenomena can be understood through this lens.

### Personal Branding

Representations of identity increasingly influence how individuals understand themselves.

The profile survives.

The self becomes harder to locate.

### Social Media Metrics

Visibility becomes a proxy for importance.

The metric structures perception.

The underlying reality becomes secondary.

### Credential Inflation

Credentials increasingly function as representations of competence.

The credential survives.

Capability becomes harder to evaluate directly.

### AI Benchmarking

Performance scores increasingly shape perceptions of capability.

The benchmark survives.

The underlying capability becomes more difficult to observe.

### Reality Drift

Representations remain active while increasingly shaping the ways individuals, institutions, and systems interpret reality itself.

## George Lakoff, Mark Johnson, and Reality Drift

Lakoff and Johnson identified a fundamental feature of cognition.

Human beings understand reality through representational frameworks.

What they did not fully witness was the emergence of digital systems capable of continuously generating, amplifying, measuring, and optimizing representations at planetary scale.

Reality Drift can be understood as an attempt to describe what happens when representational frameworks become embedded not only in language, but in institutions, platforms, algorithms, metrics, and AI systems.

The challenge is no longer merely recognizing the metaphors that shape thought.

The challenge is recognizing the representational systems that increasingly shape reality itself.
