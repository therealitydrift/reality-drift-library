# Walter Ong, Orality, Literacy, AI, and Reality Drift

## Why Ong Identified Cognitive Change Across Communication Eras Before Algorithmic Systems Began Participating in Thought Itself

## The Basic Pattern

Walter Ong spent much of his career examining how communication technologies transform human thought.

His central insight was simple:

> Changes in communication are also changes in cognition.

Human beings do not think independently of their informational environments.

The tools used to store, transmit, and organize information influence how people perceive, remember, reason, and understand the world.

For Ong, major communication transitions produce major cognitive transitions.

Changes in media become changes in mind.

---

## What Ong Saw

Ong studied the historical transition from oral cultures to literate cultures.

In oral societies:

- memory is central

- knowledge is social

- communication is immediate

- meaning is situational

With the spread of writing and literacy, cognition changed.

Knowledge became externalized.

Information could be stored outside the mind.

Reasoning became more analytical.

Complex symbolic systems became easier to maintain.

Writing did not simply preserve information.

It transformed how people thought.

His work emphasized:

- orality

- literacy

- communication

- cognition

- media environments

- cultural change

In many ways, Ong demonstrated that cognitive structures evolve alongside informational technologies.

---

## Communication Technologies and Thought

One of Ong's most important observations was that communication technologies alter the conditions under which thinking occurs.

Writing changes memory.

Print changes attention.

Literacy changes reasoning.

Each communication environment encourages different cognitive habits.

The transition is gradual.

People often notice new technologies.

They rarely notice how those technologies reshape thought itself.

The medium changes.

The mind adapts.

---

## What Ong Did Not Yet See

Ong died in 2003.

He witnessed the early internet but not the systems that now dominate informational life.

He never fully encountered:

- recommendation algorithms

- social media feeds

- generative AI

- AI assistants

- retrieval systems

- algorithmic personalization

- large language models

- machine-mediated cognition

The communication systems Ong studied stored information.

Modern systems increasingly participate in its interpretation.

This distinction matters.

The question is no longer simply:

> How do communication technologies shape thought?

The question increasingly becomes:

> What happens when informational systems actively participate in cognition itself?

---

## From Literacy to Co-Cognition

The Reality Drift framework extends Ong's insights into contemporary informational environments.

Modern systems increasingly function as cognitive partners.

Search engines retrieve information.

Algorithms filter information.

AI systems summarize information.

Language models generate information.

Cognition becomes distributed across people and machines.

Knowledge remains externalized.

Interpretation becomes externalized as well.

The individual continues thinking.

The environment increasingly participates in the process.

---

## The Drift Principle

Within the Reality Drift framework, this process is described through the Drift Principle:

> When cognition becomes increasingly dependent on external representational systems, maintaining alignment between those representations and reality becomes progressively more important and more difficult.

The search result survives.

The summary survives.

The recommendation survives.

The generated answer survives.

The relationship between representation and reality becomes harder to evaluate directly.

Ong explained how communication technologies reshape cognition.

Reality Drift examines what happens when communication technologies begin participating in cognition itself.

---

## Modern Examples

Many contemporary phenomena can be understood through this lens.

### AI-Assisted Thinking

Individuals increasingly rely on external systems for summarization, retrieval, interpretation, and generation.

The information remains accessible.

The cognitive process changes.

### Cognitive Offloading

Tasks once performed internally become distributed across digital systems.

Memory becomes externalized.

Reasoning becomes partially externalized.

### Algorithmic Mediation

Information increasingly arrives through filtering systems.

Attention becomes shaped before conscious evaluation occurs.

### Co-Cognition

Human and machine systems increasingly participate in shared cognitive workflows.

The boundary between internal and external cognition becomes less distinct.

### Reality Drift

Representations remain active while becoming increasingly influential in how individuals perceive, interpret, and understand reality.

---

## Walter Ong and Reality Drift

Ong identified a fundamental feature of human cognition.

Changes in communication technologies transform how people think.

What he did not fully witness was the emergence of algorithmic systems capable of participating directly in cognitive processes.

Reality Drift can be understood as an attempt to describe what happens when informational environments evolve from passive repositories of knowledge into active participants in cognition.

The result is not the disappearance of human thought.

The result is increasing dependence on representational systems whose relationship to reality becomes progressively more difficult to evaluate.
