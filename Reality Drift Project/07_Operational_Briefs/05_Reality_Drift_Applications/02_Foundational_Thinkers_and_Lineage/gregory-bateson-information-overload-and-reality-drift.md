## The Basic Pattern

Gregory Bateson spent much of his career studying how living systems maintain coherence.

He was interested in:

- communication

- context

- feedback

- learning

- adaptation

- systems thinking

Across biology, psychology, anthropology, and cybernetics, Bateson repeatedly returned to a similar insight:

Systems remain healthy when they preserve meaningful relationships between signals, context, and feedback.

When those relationships weaken, confusion begins to emerge.

Signals become harder to interpret.

Context becomes unstable.

Feedback loses reliability.

The system continues operating while becoming progressively less capable of accurately understanding itself and its environment.

---

## The Pattern That Connects

Bateson described this through his famous phrase:

> "The pattern that connects."

For Bateson, meaning does not exist in isolated pieces of information.

Meaning emerges from relationships.

Organisms, minds, cultures, and institutions remain coherent because they preserve patterns that connect signals to contexts and actions to consequences.

When those relationships remain intact, systems can learn.

When those relationships weaken, systems become increasingly vulnerable to distortion.

In many ways, Bateson identified one of the central mechanisms behind what would later become the Reality Drift framework.

---

## What Bateson Saw

Bateson observed several dynamics that remain highly relevant today:

- feedback failure

- context loss

- signal confusion

- runaway systems

- communication breakdown

- recursive instability

These dynamics appeared across:

- families

- organizations

- cultures

- ecological systems

- learning environments

His work repeatedly emphasized a simple concern:

When systems lose the ability to correctly interpret feedback, they begin drifting away from reality.

---

## What Bateson Did Not Yet See

Bateson died in 1980.

The informational environment that dominates modern life did not yet exist.

He never encountered:

- social media feeds

- recommendation algorithms

- engagement optimization

- infinite content streams

- algorithmic personalization

- generative AI

- synthetic media

- large-scale symbolic automation

The systems Bateson studied generated information.

Modern digital systems generate information at unprecedented scale.

This distinction matters.

The challenge is no longer merely maintaining coherence inside a system.

The challenge is maintaining coherence inside environments specifically optimized to produce more signals than systems can meaningfully integrate.

---

## From Feedback Failure to Information Overload

The Reality Drift framework extends Bateson's insights into modern informational environments.

The central question becomes:

> What happens when informational complexity grows faster than systems can maintain coherent relationships between signals, context, and feedback?

As information accelerates:

- distinctions weaken

- context becomes unstable

- feedback becomes noisy

- interpretation becomes more difficult

The system increasingly relies on simplified models, heuristics, and compressed representations.

The pattern remains.

The correspondence weakens.

---

## The Drift Principle

Within the Reality Drift framework, this process is described by the Drift Principle:

> When informational complexity increases faster than corrective mechanisms can maintain alignment, representations gradually drift away from the realities they were designed to track.

This is not a rejection of Bateson's work.

It is an attempt to extend it into conditions Bateson never experienced.

Bateson explained how systems maintain coherence.

Reality Drift examines what happens when informational environments make coherence progressively harder to maintain.

---

## Modern Examples

Many contemporary phenomena can be understood through this lens:

### Filter Fatigue

Individuals must continuously evaluate competing streams of information.

The volume of signals overwhelms available attention.

### Synthetic Realness

Representations increasingly imitate authenticity.

The signal remains persuasive.

The grounding becomes uncertain.

### Optimization Trap

Systems optimize measurable indicators rather than underlying realities.

Feedback remains active.

Correction weakens.

### Reality Drift

Systems remain operational while gradually losing correspondence with the conditions they were created to represent.

---

## Gregory Bateson and Reality Drift

Bateson identified an important mechanism before the modern environment fully existed.

He understood that coherence depends on preserving relationships between information, context, and feedback.

What he did not witness was the emergence of digital systems capable of generating, filtering, recombining, and optimizing information at planetary scale.

Reality Drift can be understood as an attempt to describe what happens when the conditions Bateson warned about become normal features of everyday life.
