# Jacques Ellul, Technique, Optimization, and Reality Drift

## The Basic Pattern

Jacques Ellul spent much of his career studying a force he called **technique**.

Technique does not simply refer to technology.

It refers to the drive toward efficiency, optimization, predictability, measurement, and control.

For Ellul, modern societies increasingly reorganize themselves around whatever method appears most efficient.

The process often unfolds gradually.

The efficient solution spreads.

Alternative approaches disappear.

Systems become optimized around measurable performance.

Over time, optimization becomes a goal in itself.

---

## What Ellul Saw

Ellul argued that modern institutions increasingly organize themselves around technical efficiency.

This tendency appears across:

- governments

- corporations

- education

- media

- healthcare

- administration

The question gradually changes.

Instead of asking:

> Is this good?

Systems increasingly ask:

> Is this efficient?

The distinction matters.

Efficiency can improve while purposes weaken.

Optimization can succeed while outcomes deteriorate.

The process remains rational.

The direction becomes uncertain.

---

## The Autonomy of Technique

One of Ellul's most important insights was that optimization often becomes self-reinforcing.

Once a system adopts a more efficient method, competing methods become increasingly difficult to justify.

Efficiency generates pressure for additional efficiency.

Optimization generates pressure for additional optimization.

The process begins serving itself.

Individuals may not intend this outcome.

Organizations may not intend this outcome.

Yet the logic continues spreading.

The system increasingly reorganizes itself around what can be optimized.

---

## What Ellul Did Not Yet See

Ellul died in 1994.

The algorithmic environment did not yet exist.

He never encountered:

- recommendation systems

- social media feeds

- engagement optimization

- machine learning

- large language models

- algorithmic ranking

- automated experimentation

- real-time optimization systems

The institutions Ellul studied optimized processes.

Modern digital systems optimize continuously.

This distinction matters.

The question is no longer simply:

> What happens when efficiency becomes dominant?

The question increasingly becomes:

> What happens when optimization becomes automated, recursive, and planetary in scale?

---

## From Technique to Optimization Systems

The Reality Drift framework extends Ellul's insights into contemporary information environments.

Today, optimization occurs everywhere.

Platforms optimize engagement.

Organizations optimize metrics.

AI systems optimize objectives.

Institutions optimize indicators.

Media systems optimize attention.

The process becomes increasingly detached from the original purpose.

The metric survives.

The purpose weakens.

The representation survives.

The reality becomes harder to observe.

---

## The Drift Principle

Within the Reality Drift framework, this process is described through the Drift Principle:

> When optimization increasingly targets representations rather than realities, systems gradually lose alignment with the conditions they were designed to serve.

This does not require corruption.

It does not require bad intentions.

It emerges naturally from optimization itself.

The dashboard improves.

The KPI improves.

The benchmark improves.

The system appears successful.

Yet correspondence with reality becomes increasingly uncertain.

Ellul described the rise of technique.

Reality Drift examines what happens when optimization systems become the dominant organizing principle of digital life.

---

## Modern Examples

Many contemporary phenomena can be understood through this lens.

### The Optimization Trap

Systems increasingly optimize measurable indicators.

The indicator improves.

The underlying objective becomes secondary.

### Social Media

Platforms optimize engagement.

Attention increases.

Meaningful interaction becomes harder to evaluate.

### KPI Distortion

Organizations optimize metrics.

Performance indicators improve.

Real-world outcomes become less certain.

### AI Alignment Failures

Systems successfully optimize objectives.

The intended purpose weakens.

### Reality Drift

Representations remain active while becoming progressively more responsive to optimization pressures than to reality itself.

## Jacques Ellul and Reality Drift

Ellul identified a critical feature of modern society before the emergence of algorithmic systems.

He understood that optimization can become autonomous.

What he did not fully witness was the rise of digital systems capable of continuously measuring, ranking, predicting, and optimizing representations at unprecedented scale.

Reality Drift can be understood as an attempt to describe what happens when the logic of technique becomes embedded inside information systems themselves.

Optimization no longer merely influences society.

Optimization increasingly shapes the representations through which society understands reality.
