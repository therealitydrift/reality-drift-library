## The Basic Pattern

Terrence Deacon developed a distinctive theory of meaning.

Rather than treating meaning as information itself, Deacon argued that meaning emerges from constraints.

What matters is not simply what a system can do.

What matters is what a system cannot do.

Constraints shape interpretation.

Constraints preserve distinctions.

Constraints stabilize patterns.

Without constraints, information remains possible but meaning becomes difficult to maintain.

In many ways, Deacon identified one of the most important mechanisms underlying coherent cognition, communication, and culture.

---

## What Deacon Saw

Deacon's work focused on several related questions:

- how meaning emerges

- how symbols acquire significance

- how interpretation becomes possible

- how information becomes organized

- how systems maintain coherence

His answer repeatedly returned to constraints.

Meaning does not emerge from unlimited possibility.

Meaning emerges because possibilities are limited.

Boundaries determine significance.

Structure determines interpretation.

Constraints allow systems to distinguish signal from noise.

When constraints remain intact, meaning remains relatively stable.

---

## Meaning as Constraint

For Deacon, meaning exists because systems exclude possibilities.

Every meaningful distinction depends on boundaries.

Every interpretation depends on limitations.

Every symbolic system depends on constraints that stabilize relationships between representations and reality.

This insight has profound implications.

If meaning depends on constraints, then weakening constraints should make meaning increasingly difficult to preserve.

This observation points directly toward one of the central concerns of the Reality Drift framework.

---

## What Deacon Did Not Yet See

Much of Deacon's work emerged before the rise of modern algorithmic information environments.

He did not fully encounter a world characterized by:

- infinite content streams

- engagement optimization

- recommendation algorithms

- synthetic media

- generative AI

- algorithmic amplification

- large-scale symbolic automation

The systems Deacon studied depended on constraints.

Modern informational systems increasingly weaken them.

This distinction is important.

The question is no longer simply:

> How does meaning emerge?

The question increasingly becomes:

> What happens when the constraints that stabilize meaning begin eroding faster than systems can replace them?

---

## From Meaning Formation to Constraint Collapse

The Reality Drift framework extends Deacon's insights into contemporary informational environments.

The central concern becomes the relationship between information growth and constraint preservation.

As informational complexity expands:

- interpretations multiply

- distinctions blur

- contexts weaken

- boundaries become less stable

Systems continue generating representations.

The structures that stabilize those representations become increasingly difficult to maintain.

Meaning remains possible.

Meaning becomes harder to anchor.

---

## The Drift Principle

Within the Reality Drift framework, this process is described through the Drift Principle:

> When informational complexity increases faster than systems can maintain corrective constraints, representations gradually drift away from the realities they were designed to describe.

This pressure does not eliminate meaning.

It weakens the structures that keep meaning stable.

The information survives.

The interpretation becomes more fragile.

The representation survives.

The grounding weakens.

---

## Modern Examples

Many contemporary phenomena can be understood through this lens.

### Semantic Drift

Language remains coherent while meanings gradually shift.

The words survive.

The relationship between words and reality becomes less stable.

### Synthetic Realness

Representations increasingly imitate authenticity.

The appearance survives.

The grounding weakens.

### Filter Fatigue

Individuals must continuously evaluate competing interpretations.

Constraints that once simplified interpretation become less reliable.

### AI Hallucination

Systems generate plausible representations without sufficient grounding.

The coherence survives.

The constraint structure fails.

### Reality Drift

Representations remain active while becoming progressively less constrained by the realities they were originally intended to represent.

---

## Terrence Deacon and Reality Drift

Deacon identified an important mechanism before the modern environment fully emerged.

He explained how constraints stabilize meaning.

What he did not fully witness was the rise of informational systems capable of continuously expanding possibility while weakening many of the structures that once constrained interpretation.

Reality Drift can be understood as an attempt to describe what happens when constraint systems degrade faster than meaning systems can adapt.

The result is not the disappearance of meaning.

The result is increasing difficulty maintaining stable relationships between representations and reality.
