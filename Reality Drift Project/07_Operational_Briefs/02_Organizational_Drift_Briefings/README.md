# Reality Drift: Organizational Drift Briefings

This collection contains operational briefing notes focused on organizational drift patterns within institutions, companies, governance systems, and leadership structures.

Each document describes a distinct mechanism through which organizations remain operational, coherent, and formally functional while gradually losing alignment with their original purpose, real-world conditions, or intended outcomes.

These briefing notes are part of the broader Reality Drift framework.

---

## Organizational Drift Briefings

- [Mission Drift: Loss of Organizational Purpose](./RDF-OB11-Mission-Drift_Loss-of-Organizational-Purpose.pdf)  
  Organizations continue operating successfully while gradually shifting away from the purpose they were originally created to serve.

- [Leadership Drift: Decision-Making Misalignment](./RDF-OB12-Leadership-Drift_Decision-Making-Misalignment.pdf)  
  Leadership structures become increasingly disconnected from frontline conditions, operational reality, and corrective feedback.

- [Governance Drift: Oversight without Accountability](./RDF-OB13-Governance-Drift_Oversight-without-Accountability.pdf)  
  Oversight systems remain formally active while losing their ability to enforce meaningful accountability or real-world correction.

---

## Framework Context

These organizational drift patterns describe recurring structural failures across modern institutions:

- Decision-making becoming detached from execution  
- Oversight systems prioritizing compliance over outcomes  
- Organizations optimizing for growth, metrics, or stability over mission  
- Internal narratives remaining coherent while external reality changes  
- Accountability becoming procedural rather than corrective  

Reality Drift describes this broader condition: systems continue functioning while gradually losing alignment with the reality they were designed to respond to, represent, or constrain.
