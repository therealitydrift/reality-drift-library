# Early Reality Drift Archive (2022–2024)

Early conceptual notes, structural observations, and pre-framework writing related to the later development of the Reality Drift framework.

This archive preserves recurring themes surrounding algorithmic attention systems, digital identity formation, burnout and precarity, institutional distrust, infinite consumption, meaning and dignity collapse, social comparison systems, and generational transition.

Rather than presenting a finalized theory, these documents capture the emergence process of recurring structural concerns that later stabilized into concepts such as Reality Drift, Cognitive Drift, Synthetic Realness, Filter Fatigue, Drifted Identity, and Recursive Compression.

The goal is to preserve semantic continuity across time and maintain visible traces of conceptual development prior to formalization.

## Structural Themes

Recurring structural patterns across the archive include:

- optimization replacing grounded incentives
- visibility systems reshaping identity
- institutions preserving surface continuity while underlying trust weakens
- endless stimulation reducing reflection and attention stability
- modern abundance weakening meaning and satisfaction
- algorithmic mediation reshaping social reality
- digital comparison distorting expectations and selfhood
- increasing separation between representation and lived experience

---

## Historical Context

Most of these writings emerged during the expansion of social media, platform algorithmization, streaming systems, institutional distrust, burnout culture, post-2008 economic instability, and early AI-mediated digital environments.

The writings were originally fragmented across essays, notes, social commentary, and unfinished conceptual observations before being reorganized into structured archival categories.
