## Abstract

Conceptual frameworks are often criticized for lacking empirical precision during their early stages of development. This criticism can be valid, but it can also confuse conceptual architecture with operational measurement. Reality Drift begins as a structural model rather than an experimental one. Its primary function is to identify a recurring pattern in which systems maintain operational continuity while their alignment with reality degrades. This paper examines how such a framework can be evaluated without collapsing it into overly narrow metrics. The goal is not to produce a single measurement for drift, but to identify observable patterns that indicate its presence across systems.

## Conceptual Models and Measurement

Not all frameworks begin as empirical instruments. Many begin by clarifying distinctions, isolating recurring patterns, and providing orientation for later measurement. This is common across scientific, institutional, and philosophical history. Reality Drift belongs to this category. Its initial function is diagnostic. It identifies a structural condition before proposing fixed measurement systems.

This distinction matters because critics often demand immediate quantification as proof of legitimacy. While quantification is valuable, conceptual clarity often precedes operational formalization. The absence of a single metric does not imply the absence of a real pattern. It often means the framework is still at the stage of identifying the dimensions that require measurement.

## Proxy Divergence

One of the clearest observable markers of Reality Drift is divergence between proxies and the realities they are intended to track. This occurs when performance indicators improve while real-world outcomes stagnate or degrade. The gap between metric success and experiential or operational reality is one of the strongest empirical signals of drift.

This pattern appears in AI benchmarks, healthcare systems, institutional compliance systems, and customer service environments. In each case, the proxy remains functional and often improves, while the underlying condition weakens. Reality Drift predicts that this divergence becomes more common as systems scale and optimize around their own representations.

## Benchmark Versus Real-World Performance

In artificial intelligence, one of the most measurable domains of Reality Drift appears in benchmark performance. Models may achieve increasingly strong scores on controlled evaluation tasks while performing unpredictably in real-world environments. This is not simply a failure of intelligence. It is often a failure of alignment between the benchmark and the complexity of reality.

Reality Drift predicts that benchmark success can conceal growing fragility when the evaluation environment becomes increasingly abstracted from deployment conditions. This creates an observable pattern in which formal competence rises while practical fidelity becomes unstable.

## Semantic Fidelity Decay

Another evaluable marker of Reality Drift is semantic fidelity decay. This occurs when meaning degrades across recursive transformations while surface coherence remains intact. In language models, this can be observed through paraphrasing, summarization, retrieval pipelines, or multi-agent workflows where outputs remain fluent but progressively lose intent, nuance, or contextual precision.

This matters because it demonstrates drift at the representational level. The system continues functioning. The language remains coherent. But the relationship between the output and the original meaning weakens. This is one of the clearest examples of operational continuity masking alignment loss.

## Institutional Survivable Wrongness

Reality Drift can also be evaluated through institutional persistence under conditions of visible misalignment. This occurs when organizations continue operating despite recurring evidence that their procedures no longer accurately track the conditions they are meant to manage.

Examples include healthcare administration, regulatory systems, education metrics, and corporate performance structures. In these cases, failure does not trigger correction because the system remains operationally viable. The ability to survive being wrong becomes part of the structure itself.

This pattern is not always easy to quantify, but it can be observed historically and operationally.

## Recursive Compression Failure

A further indicator of drift is recursive compression failure. This occurs when repeated abstraction reduces fidelity faster than corrective feedback can restore it. In modern systems, information increasingly passes through layers of summarization, categorization, translation, and optimization. Each transformation compresses reality into more manageable symbolic forms.

Reality Drift predicts that under sufficient recursion, these compressed representations begin referencing one another rather than the underlying conditions they originally described. The result is increasing coherence inside the system alongside decreasing correspondence outside it.

This pattern is increasingly visible in AI-mediated environments.

## Toward Operational Models

Reality Drift does not currently propose a universal metric. Instead, it proposes a family of observable indicators that can be operationalized differently across domains. This is consistent with the nature of structural models. Different systems drift in different ways.

The task of future work is not to force a single measurement onto drift, but to develop domain-specific diagnostics that preserve the structural distinctions of the framework. This may include semantic audits, proxy divergence analysis, recursive fidelity tests, and survivability mapping.

These are not external additions to the framework. They are natural extensions of it.

## Conclusion

Reality Drift is not beyond evaluation. It is simply earlier in its developmental arc than many of the systems it describes. Its claims generate observable consequences, and those consequences can be studied across multiple domains. The challenge is methodological discipline. Evaluation must preserve the structural distinctions of the framework rather than reducing it into narrower existing categories.

The goal is not to prove drift through one number. The goal is to identify the conditions under which systems continue working while becoming progressively less aligned with reality. That condition is increasingly measurable, even if its forms remain domain-specific.
