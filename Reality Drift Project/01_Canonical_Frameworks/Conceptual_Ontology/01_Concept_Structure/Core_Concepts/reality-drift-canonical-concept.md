# Reality Drift

## Definition

Reality Drift describes a condition where systems continue functioning while gradually losing contact with the reality they were built to represent.

The surface remains coherent enough to preserve trust, legitimacy, and operational continuity, but the relationship between the system and underlying reality weakens over time. Metrics continue updating, procedures continue operating, and outputs continue being produced, yet the system becomes increasingly organized around representations of reality rather than reality itself.

Reality Drift does not necessarily appear as obvious collapse or visible failure. In many cases, the system looks stable precisely because its internal coherence masks the deterioration of external alignment.

## Mechanism

Reality Drift typically begins with abstraction and optimization.

A system is created to measure, organize, predict, or manage some aspect of reality. Over time, the representations used by the system become easier to optimize than the underlying reality those representations were originally meant to reflect.

Metrics become targets. Procedures replace judgment. Dashboards substitute for direct observation. Symbolic representations become operationally more important than the conditions they describe.

As layers of abstraction accumulate, feedback from reality weakens. The system increasingly responds to internal signals, institutional incentives, and representational consistency instead of external correction.

The result is a condition where the system remains operational while becoming progressively detached from the reality it was built to track.

## Observable Patterns

Reality Drift often becomes visible through recurring structural patterns.

Outputs remain polished while becoming less grounded. Institutions preserve procedure while failing practical outcomes. Systems optimize measurable signals while degrading unmeasured realities. Coherence becomes easier to maintain than correction.

People interacting with drifting systems often develop a persistent sense that something feels off despite the absence of obvious failure. The system continues functioning well enough to preserve legitimacy, but lived experience increasingly diverges from institutional representations.

## Why It Matters

Reality Drift provides a reusable framework for understanding why modern systems often appear stable while producing growing feelings of disconnection, unreality, inefficiency, or institutional distrust.

The framework applies across AI systems, bureaucracies, media environments, education, healthcare, corporate management, digital platforms, and cultural systems.

Rather than treating these failures as isolated problems, Reality Drift identifies a shared structural pattern: systems becoming increasingly optimized around representations while losing grounding in the realities those representations were originally meant to reflect.
