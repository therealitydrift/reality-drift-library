# The Age of Drift

## Overview

Modern systems increasingly remain functional while losing contact with the realities they were designed to represent.

The Age of Drift introduces the core concepts of the Reality Drift framework, examining how optimization, compression, abstraction, and digital mediation reshape institutions, information systems, cognition, and everyday life. Across organizations, algorithms, media environments, and artificial intelligence systems, representations increasingly replace direct contact with reality while operational continuity remains intact.

This explainer serves as an introduction to Reality Drift, Constraint Collapse, Recursive Compression, Synthetic Realness, Semantic Fidelity, Filter Fatigue, Cognitive Drift, and the Drifted Self.

## In One Sentence

Reality Drift is the condition in which systems remain coherent and operational while gradually losing alignment with the realities they were created to represent.

---

## Main Takeaways

### 1. Modern Systems Depend On Representations

Dashboards, metrics, models, algorithms, summaries, and interfaces allow complex systems to scale.

### 2. Compression Enables Coordination

Reality must be simplified into representations before it can be managed, measured, and optimized.

### 3. Optimization Creates Drift

As systems optimize representations, the connection between representation and reality can weaken.

### 4. Drift Appears Across Multiple Layers

Institutions, media systems, AI systems, and individual cognition all experience forms of drift.

### 5. Regrounding Matters More Than Optimization

The challenge is not producing more information, activity, or efficiency. The challenge is preserving meaningful contact with reality.

## Why This Matters

Many contemporary problems appear unrelated on the surface.

Institutional dysfunction, AI hallucinations, information overload, algorithmic feeds, burnout, identity fragmentation, and declining trust often emerge from the same underlying pattern: representations becoming increasingly detached from the realities they were intended to represent.

The Age of Drift provides a framework for understanding these phenomena as expressions of a shared structural condition.
