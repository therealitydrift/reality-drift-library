# Old Thinkers and the Intellectual Foundations of Reality Drift

This collection explores thinkers whose work helps illuminate different dimensions of Reality Drift. While none of these authors used the term, each identified mechanisms through which representations, institutions, media systems, technologies, and symbolic environments become increasingly detached from the realities they were intended to reflect.

## Papers

### Alfred Korzybski

- [Map, Territory, and Reality Drift](./alfred-korzybski-map-territory-and-reality-drift.md)

Explores how representations become mistaken for reality itself and why maintaining awareness of the distinction is essential for preserving semantic fidelity.

### Erich Fromm

- [Alienation, Authenticity, and Reality Drift](./erich-fromm-alienation-authenticity-and-reality-drift.md)

Examines how modern social systems encourage adaptation to external expectations at the expense of authentic human experience.

### George Lakoff & Mark Johnson

- [Conceptual Metaphors and Reality Drift](./george-lakoff-mark-johnson-conceptual-metaphors-and-reality-drift.md)

Investigates how metaphors shape cognition and how inherited conceptual structures influence the way reality is interpreted.

### Gregory Bateson

- [Information Overload and Reality Drift](./gregory-bateson-information-overload-and-reality-drift.md)

Explores information, feedback, and ecological thinking as tools for understanding drift within complex systems.

### Ivan Illich

- [Institutional Counterproductivity and Reality Drift](./ivan-illich-institutional-counterproductivity-and-reality-drift.md)

Shows how institutions can become increasingly effective at sustaining themselves while becoming less effective at achieving their original purpose.

### Jacques Ellul

- [Technique, Optimization, and Reality Drift](./jacques-ellul-technique-optimization-and-reality-drift.md)

Examines how optimization becomes a governing logic that reshapes institutions, technology, and culture.

### Lewis Mumford

- [Technical Systems and Reality Drift](./lewis-mumford-technical-systems-and-reality-drift.md)

Analyzes how large technical systems alter human behavior and social organization.

### Marshall McLuhan

- [Algorithmic Media and Reality Drift](./marshall-mcluhan-algorithmic-media-and-reality-drift.md)

Explores how media environments shape perception and why changes in communication systems transform society itself.

### Humberto Maturana & Francisco Varela

- [Autopoiesis and Reality Drift](./maturana-varela-autopoiesis-and-reality-drift.md)

Investigates self-producing systems and how closed feedback loops can become increasingly detached from external reality.

### Max Weber

- [Rationalization, Bureaucracy, and Reality Drift](./max-weber-rationalization-bureaucracy-and-reality-drift.md)

Examines how bureaucratic systems gradually prioritize procedural logic over substantive outcomes.

### Neil Postman

- [Entertainment Culture and Reality Drift](./neil-postman-entertainment-culture-and-reality-drift.md)

Explores how media incentives reshape public discourse and alter the conditions under which meaning is produced.

### Niklas Luhmann

- [Self-Referential Systems and Reality Drift](./niklas-luhmann-self-referential-systems-and-reality-drift.md)

Investigates how complex social systems increasingly operate according to their own internal logic rather than direct contact with reality.

### Norbert Wiener

- [Cybernetics, Feedback, and Reality Drift](./norbert-wiener-cybernetics-feedback-and-reality-drift.md)

Provides foundational concepts for understanding control systems, feedback loops, and adaptive behavior.

### Robert Rosen

- [Anticipatory Systems and Reality Drift](./robert-rosen-anticipatory-systems-and-reality-drift.md)

Explores systems that act on internal models of reality and the challenges that emerge when models diverge from conditions on the ground.

### Terrence Deacon

- [Constraint Collapse and Reality Drift](./terrence-deacon-constraint-collapse-and-reality-drift.md)

Examines how constraints create meaning and organization, and what happens when those constraints weaken.

### Walter Ong

- [Orality, Literacy, AI, and Reality Drift](./walter-ong-orality-literacy-ai-and-reality-drift.md)

Explores how communication technologies reshape cognition and how AI may represent another major shift in symbolic culture.

### Yuri Lotman

- [Semiosphere and Reality Drift](./yuri-lotman-semiosphere-and-reality-drift.md)

Investigates how symbolic environments evolve and how meaning is produced, maintained, and transformed within cultural systems.

## Common Themes

Across these thinkers, several recurring themes emerge:

- representations shaping reality
- media environments influencing cognition
- institutional self-preservation
- optimization and technique
- feedback loops and cybernetics
- symbolic systems and meaning
- self-reference and system closure
- constraint loss and organizational drift
- authenticity and alienation
- model–reality divergence

Together these works provide intellectual foundations for understanding Reality Drift as a broader pattern that appears across technological, institutional, cultural, and cognitive systems.
