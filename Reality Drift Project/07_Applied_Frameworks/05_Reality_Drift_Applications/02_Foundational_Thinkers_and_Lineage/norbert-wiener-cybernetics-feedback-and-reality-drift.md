# Norbert Wiener, Cybernetics, Feedback, and Reality Drift

## The Basic Pattern

Norbert Wiener is widely regarded as the founder of cybernetics.

His central question was deceptively simple:

> How do systems remain aligned with their objectives in changing environments?

Whether examining organisms, machines, institutions, or societies, Wiener observed that successful systems depend upon feedback.

Systems act.

The environment responds.

The system receives information about the result.

Behavior adjusts.

The cycle repeats.

Without feedback, correction becomes impossible.

Without correction, drift becomes inevitable.

---

## What Wiener Saw

Wiener recognized that communication and control are fundamentally connected.

A system cannot maintain direction unless it receives reliable information about its relationship to reality.

This principle appears across:

- biological systems

- engineering systems

- organizations

- governments

- communication networks

In each case, successful adaptation depends upon the continuous flow of corrective information.

His work emphasized:

- cybernetics

- communication

- control systems

- feedback loops

- adaptation

- information theory

In many ways, Wiener identified one of the foundational mechanisms underlying all self-correcting systems.

---

## Feedback and Control

For Wiener, control does not emerge from perfect knowledge.

Control emerges from correction.

A system does not need to know everything.

It only needs sufficiently accurate feedback to recognize when it has moved away from its intended state.

The process is continuous.

Action produces consequences.

Consequences produce information.

Information produces adjustment.

The relationship between system and environment remains synchronized through feedback.

This insight would become foundational across systems theory, cybernetics, artificial intelligence, organizational theory, and communications research.

---

## What Wiener Did Not Yet See

Wiener died in 1964.

The modern information environment did not yet exist.

He never encountered:

- social media platforms

- recommendation algorithms

- engagement optimization

- information overload

- algorithmic personalization

- synthetic media

- generative AI

- large-scale digital communication systems

The systems Wiener studied depended upon feedback.

Modern environments increasingly distort, delay, filter, overwhelm, and optimize feedback.

This distinction matters.

The question is no longer simply:

> How do systems remain aligned through feedback?

The question increasingly becomes:

> What happens when feedback becomes less capable of correcting drift?

---

## From Feedback to Feedback Saturation

The Reality Drift framework extends Wiener's insights into contemporary informational environments.

Modern systems encounter unprecedented volumes of information.

Signals multiply.

Representations multiply.

Communication accelerates.

The amount of available feedback expands dramatically.

Paradoxically, correction often becomes more difficult.

Not because feedback disappears.

Because feedback becomes increasingly difficult to interpret.

The system receives more information.

The signal becomes harder to distinguish from the noise.

---

## The Drift Principle

Within the Reality Drift framework, this process is described through the Drift Principle:

> When informational complexity increases faster than corrective mechanisms can maintain alignment, representations gradually drift away from the realities they were designed to track.

This process does not require deception.

It does not require failure.

It emerges when correction becomes less effective than change.

The system continues operating.

The feedback remains active.

Alignment gradually weakens.

Wiener explained how systems maintain control through feedback.

Reality Drift examines what happens when informational environments increasingly overwhelm the feedback systems responsible for correction.

---

## Modern Examples

Many contemporary phenomena can be understood through this lens.

### Information Overload

Individuals receive unprecedented amounts of information.

The volume of feedback increases.

The ability to interpret it weakens.

### KPI Distortion

Organizations increasingly rely on indirect indicators.

The feedback remains visible.

The underlying reality becomes harder to observe.

### AI Alignment

Models receive optimization signals through objectives and evaluations.

Performance improves.

Alignment remains uncertain.

### Social Media

Platforms continuously optimize engagement based on feedback loops.

The feedback survives.

The purpose becomes less clear.

### Reality Drift

Systems remain operational while becoming progressively less capable of using available feedback to maintain alignment with reality.

---

## Norbert Wiener and Reality Drift

Wiener identified one of the most important principles underlying adaptive systems.

He understood that feedback enables correction.

What he did not fully witness was the emergence of environments capable of generating more information than correction systems can meaningfully integrate.

Reality Drift can be understood as an attempt to describe what happens when informational acceleration begins outpacing corrective capacity.

The result is not the disappearance of feedback.

The result is the gradual weakening of feedback's ability to maintain alignment.
