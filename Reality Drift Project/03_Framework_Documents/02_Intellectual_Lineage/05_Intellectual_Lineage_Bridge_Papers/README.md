# Reality Drift Foundations

Five intellectual lineage papers connecting the Reality Drift framework to earlier work on representation, alienation, cognitive framing, feedback, information overload, and institutional counterproductivity.

Each paper begins with a foundational thinker and extends their ideas into modern systems shaped by metrics, platforms, optimization, automation, and recursive feedback.

## Papers

### [Alfred Korzybski, the Map-Territory Distinction, and Reality Drift](alfred-korzybski-map-territory-proxy-optimization-reality-drift.pdf)

Examines what happens when metrics, models, dashboards, benchmarks, and categories become targets rather than tools for observing reality. Connects the map-territory distinction to proxy optimization, Goodhart’s Law, metric gaming, benchmark overfitting, and representational drift.

### [Erich Fromm, Alienation, Authenticity, and Reality Drift](erich-fromm-alienation-authenticity-drifted-self-reality-drift.pdf)

Explores how social platforms, personal branding, audience feedback, and algorithmic visibility can turn identity into a continuous performance. Connects Fromm’s work on alienation and conformity to identity optimization, synthetic realness, and the Drifted Self.

### [George Lakoff, Mark Johnson, Conceptual Metaphors, and Reality Drift](george-lakoff-mark-johnson-conceptual-metaphors-representational-environments-reality-drift.pdf)

Shows how metaphors, categories, interfaces, rankings, and metrics shape perception before conscious judgment begins. Extends conceptual metaphor theory into digital representational environments that influence what appears valuable, relevant, intelligent, or real.

### [Gregory Bateson, Feedback, Information Overload, and Reality Drift](gregory-bateson-feedback-information-overload-reality-drift.pdf)

Reframes information overload as a breakdown in the relationships that give signals meaning. Connects Bateson’s work on context, cybernetics, and feedback to signal saturation, filter fatigue, recommendation loops, generative AI, and informational drift.

### [Ivan Illich, Institutional Counterproductivity, and Reality Drift](ivan-illich-institutional-counterproductivity-representational-optimization-reality-drift.pdf)

Examines how institutions can remain active while becoming less capable of serving their original purpose. Connects institutional counterproductivity to administrative burden, professional dependence, credentialism, service drift, and representational optimization.

## Shared Pattern

Across these papers, the same structural sequence repeatedly appears:

1. Reality is reduced into a representation.
2. The representation becomes embedded in decisions.
3. Incentives form around what the system can measure.
4. Behavior adapts to the representation.
5. Feedback increasingly reflects the system’s own structure.
6. Correspondence with reality weakens while the system remains operational.

Together, these papers trace several of the intellectual foundations beneath the Reality Drift framework.
