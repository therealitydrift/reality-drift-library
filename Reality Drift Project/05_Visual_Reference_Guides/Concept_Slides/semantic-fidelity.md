# Semantic Fidelity

Part of the Reality Drift framework (2023–2026) by A. Jacobs

## Canonical Definition

Semantic Fidelity refers to the degree to which meaning is preserved as information moves through systems of representation.

In high-fidelity environments, symbols remain anchored to the realities they describe. Language, models, and narratives continue to track the phenomena they were originally meant to represent.

When semantic fidelity declines, representations remain coherent and intelligible but gradually diverge from their underlying reference. Meaning becomes increasingly detached from the realities it once described.

## What Makes It Distinct

Many theories of information focus on transmission accuracy — whether signals arrive intact.

Semantic Fidelity focuses instead on meaning preservation.

A system can transmit signals perfectly while still losing semantic fidelity if the relationship between representation and reference weakens.

This distinction becomes critical in complex symbolic environments where:

- language circulates faster than it can be verified
- models refine internal coherence faster than external validation
- narratives persist even after losing grounding in lived experience

Semantic Fidelity therefore measures the stability of meaning rather than the reliability of transmission.

## Mechanism

Semantic fidelity degrades when the processes that bind representation to reality weaken.

Several dynamics commonly contribute:

**Abstraction Acceleration**

As systems scale, representations become increasingly abstract. Abstraction improves coordination but reduces direct contact with the phenomena being represented.

**Recursive Representation**

Symbols increasingly reference other symbols rather than underlying events. Over time the system becomes internally coherent but externally decoupled.

**Optimization of Representation**

Systems begin optimizing representations for efficiency, engagement, or fluency rather than fidelity to underlying reality.

**Constraint Weakening**

Feedback mechanisms that once corrected misrepresentation weaken or slow, allowing divergence to accumulate.

When these dynamics interact, systems can maintain coherent narratives and functional communication even while semantic fidelity declines.

## Observable Patterns

Declining semantic fidelity produces recognizable patterns:

- language that feels fluent but vague  
- concepts that circulate widely while becoming less precise  
- metrics that remain stable even as underlying phenomena shift  
- narratives that persist despite contradictory evidence  
- explanations that become increasingly self-referential

Meaning remains legible, but its connection to underlying reality grows weaker.

## Relationship to Reality Drift

Within the Reality Drift framework, Semantic Fidelity describes the stability of meaning within symbolic systems.

The Drift Principle describes how increasing environmental complexity can destabilize representations. As entropy rises faster than compression capacity, representations begin to lose fidelity. 

Reality Drift emerges when systems continue operating while semantic fidelity gradually declines. Institutions, technologies, and cultures remain coherent, but their representations become progressively detached from lived reality.

Constraint Collapse represents the structural failure mode that can follow when declining semantic fidelity weakens the corrective mechanisms that once forced alignment between representation and consequence. 

## Cross-Domain Effects

Semantic fidelity can weaken across many domains.

**Institutions**

Policies and indicators persist while drifting away from the conditions they were designed to track.

**Technology**

Algorithmic systems refine models internally while their outputs gradually diverge from user intent or real-world context.

**Culture**

Language and narratives circulate widely while losing precision and grounding.

**AI Systems**

Large language models generate fluent outputs that remain internally coherent even when external grounding is limited.

## In One Sentence

Semantic Fidelity is the degree to which representations preserve their connection to the realities they are meant to describe.
