# Why Is the Internet Getting Worse?

Part of the Reality Drift framework (2023–2026) by A. Jacobs.

## The Experience

Many people feel that the internet has changed in ways that are difficult to describe but easy to recognize.

Search results feel less useful. Social media feeds feel repetitive or manipulative. Comment sections feel more hostile or performative. Even content that once felt spontaneous now often feels engineered.

People sometimes describe the internet as becoming “worse,” even though the technology itself continues to improve.

Platforms are faster, content is abundant, and tools are more powerful than ever. Yet the experience often feels more frustrating and less meaningful.

This raises a common question: **why does the internet seem to be getting worse over time?**

## What Is Happening

One explanation is that digital systems increasingly optimize for measurable outcomes rather than for user experience.

Platforms compete for attention, engagement, and growth. As these metrics become targets, systems are designed to maximize them as efficiently as possible.

Content that generates strong reactions spreads more easily. Formats that retain attention are repeated and amplified. Algorithms refine patterns that keep users interacting.

Over time, this optimization changes the environment itself.

Within the Reality Drift framework, this pattern is closely related to the Optimization Trap.

The Optimization Trap occurs when systems optimize internal performance indicators such as engagement, clicks, and growth rather than the broader outcomes those metrics were originally meant to represent.

## Connection to Reality Drift

The sense that the internet is deteriorating can be understood as a form of Reality Drift.

Reality Drift occurs when systems remain operational while gradually losing alignment with the real-world conditions they were originally designed to reflect.

Several related dynamics contribute to this shift.

**Optimization Trap**  
Platforms increasingly optimize for engagement metrics rather than meaningful interaction.

**Synthetic Realness**  
Content and communication styles are engineered to appear authentic and emotionally resonant.

**Filter Fatigue**  
Users must evaluate enormous volumes of information and signals while navigating algorithmically curated environments.

Together these forces reshape the digital landscape. The system continues functioning, but the experience gradually feels less aligned with genuine communication or discovery.

## Simple Mechanism

Engagement Optimization  
↓  
Algorithmic Amplification  
↓  
Repetitive and Reactive Content  
↓  
Perceived Decline in Quality
