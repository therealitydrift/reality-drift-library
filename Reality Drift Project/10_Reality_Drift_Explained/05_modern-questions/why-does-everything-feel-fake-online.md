# Why Does Everything Feel Fake Online?

Part of the Reality Drift framework (2023–2026) by A. Jacobs.

## The Experience

Many people have started asking a similar question: **why does everything feel fake online?**

Posts look polished but predictable. Influencers share emotional stories that feel rehearsed. Customer service replies sound warm and human but oddly impersonal. Even casual conversations online sometimes feel scripted.

Nothing appears obviously fake. In fact, most of it looks extremely convincing.

But something about the experience feels staged.

This subtle mismatch between appearance and authenticity has become a common feature of modern digital life.

## What Is Happening

One reason the internet can feel artificial is that authenticity itself has become something systems try to engineer.

Platforms increasingly optimize content for engagement, relatability, and emotional resonance. The goal is to make content feel natural and human so people interact with it more.

Over time this produces a strange effect. The signals that once indicated authenticity — tone, vulnerability, personal storytelling, casual aesthetics — become easier to replicate.

Content can appear genuine even when it follows highly optimized patterns.

Within the Reality Drift framework, environments where systems are designed to simulate authenticity are described as Synthetic Realness.

Synthetic Realness refers to situations where experiences are engineered to feel real, human, and emotionally meaningful even when they are shaped primarily by optimization and algorithmic systems.

## Connection to Reality Drift

The feeling that the internet has become strangely artificial is one everyday example of a broader condition called Reality Drift.

Reality Drift occurs when systems remain operational while gradually losing alignment with real-world context and feedback.

Several dynamics contribute to this pattern online.

**Synthetic Realness**  
Platforms increasingly design experiences that mimic human tone, authenticity, and emotional connection.

**Optimization Trap**  
Systems optimize for measurable signals such as engagement, visibility, and interaction rather than deeper meaning or context.

**Filter Fatigue**  
Users encounter enormous volumes of optimized content and must constantly evaluate what feels genuine, relevant, or meaningful.

As these dynamics intensify, content remains fluent and convincing even as authenticity becomes harder to recognize.

## Simple Mechanism

Optimization Pressure  
↓  
Synthetic Realness  
↓  
Filter Fatigue  
↓  
Reality Drift
