## What Is the Attention Economy?

The attention economy describes digital environments in which platforms compete to capture and retain user attention. Engagement signals such as clicks, views, shares, and time-on-platform become central indicators guiding platform design and algorithmic decisions.

Because attention can be measured and optimized, many digital systems prioritize content and interactions that maximize engagement. Over time this creates strong incentives to amplify information that attracts attention rather than information that accurately reflects underlying reality.

## How the Attention Economy Relates to Reality Drift

Within the **Reality Drift framework**, the attention economy alters the feedback systems that guide digital platforms.

Instead of responding primarily to real-world accuracy, usefulness, or context, platforms increasingly respond to engagement signals such as clicks, shares, and viewing time. These become the system’s primary indicators of success.

This dynamic can produce an **Optimization Trap**, where systems optimize measurable engagement metrics rather than the informational goals those systems were originally meant to support.

To capture engagement more effectively, content formats evolve to simulate authenticity, emotional presence, and human relatability. This contributes to environments of **Synthetic Realness**, where expressions of authenticity themselves become increasingly engineered.

As these optimized content streams multiply, users must constantly evaluate posts, notifications, and recommendations competing for attention, producing conditions of **Filter Fatigue**.

Over time the system can remain highly active and internally successful according to engagement metrics while the information environment it produces becomes progressively shaped by attention incentives rather than by alignment with reality.

Within the framework, this condition is described as **Reality Drift**.

## Where This Pattern Appears

These dynamics can appear across many modern digital environments:

- Social media platforms prioritizing engagement signals  
- Recommendation systems designed to maximize viewing time  
- Online news ecosystems shaped by click-driven incentives  
- Digital platforms competing for continuous user attention  

In these environments, systems can remain highly functional while the information they produce becomes progressively shaped by engagement optimization rather than reality alignment.
