# Reality Drift in Healthcare

## Why Healthcare Feels More Complex Than Ever

**Reality Drift Diagnostic #2**  
**A. Jacobs | Reality Drift Framework (2023–2026)**

---

## Introduction

Healthcare often appears to be working. The systems are advanced, the data is extensive, and the metrics continue to improve. Hospitals track performance, outcomes are measured, and decisions are increasingly data-driven. And yet something still feels off.

Patients feel unseen as care becomes fragmented and time compressed, leaving the experience of being treated out of step with the sophistication of the system providing it.

Healthcare continues to function, but its alignment with patient experience is gradually weakening.

---

## The Core Shift

Healthcare systems rely on measurement to operate at scale. Over time, those measurements become metrics, and those metrics begin to guide decisions.

As this process continues, systems begin optimizing representations of health rather than health itself. **When documentation becomes care, the patient becomes secondary.**

---

## The Representation Stack

Healthcare systems translate patient health into measurable and comparable forms through a series of layered representations. Each layer compresses the underlying reality, enabling scale while introducing loss, distortion, and error.

### Layer 0: Reality

Patient health reflects the actual condition of the patient, including physical, mental, and contextual factors.

### Layer 1: Measurement

Tests, vitals, and diagnostics capture aspects of patient health through observable data.  
**Loss:** Context, lived experience, complexity.  
**Distortion:** Only what can be measured is treated as real.  
**Error enters:** Selection—what is measured vs what is ignored.

### Layer 2: Metrics

KPIs, billing codes, and clinical indicators aggregate measurements into standardized indicators.  
**Loss:** Nuance, edge cases, qualitative differences.  
**Distortion:** Complex health states are reduced to categories.  
**Error enters:** Proxies—metrics stand in for health.

### Layer 3: Optimization

Throughput, efficiency, reimbursement, and compliance become targets.  
**Loss:** Patient well-being as a whole.  
**Distortion:** Success becomes defined by metrics.  
**Error enters:** Proxy optimization.

### Layer 4: Representation

Dashboards, reports, and system outputs present healthcare performance.  
**Loss:** Process visibility, uncertainty, variation.  
**Distortion:** Outputs appear complete regardless of gaps.  
**Error enters:** Presentation.

### Layer 5: Narrative

“High-quality care,” “improved outcomes,” and “efficient system” become narratives.  
**Loss:** Contradictions, unresolved issues, patient experience.  
**Distortion:** Coherent narratives replace reality.  
**Error enters:** Interpretation.

---

## Where Drift Emerges

Drift appears when:

- metrics become the target  
- efficiency replaces attention  
- documentation replaces interaction  
- billing structures shape care  

---

## What It Looks Like in Practice

Appointments feel increasingly rushed, even as efficiency improves. More time is spent documenting care than delivering it. Patients move through protocols that are technically correct but often leave them feeling unheard. Outcomes may appear acceptable on paper, yet feel inconsistent in experience.

---

## Why It Persists

This condition is reinforced by:

- reimbursement models  
- regulatory requirements  
- scalability needs  
- institutional incentives  

---

## The Diagnostic Question

**Are we optimizing the representation of health, or health itself?**

---

## Closing

Healthcare can continue improving its metrics while drifting from the lived reality of patients. As compression accumulates across layers, distance from the underlying condition increases.

**Systems remain operational long after they stop being answerable to reality.**
