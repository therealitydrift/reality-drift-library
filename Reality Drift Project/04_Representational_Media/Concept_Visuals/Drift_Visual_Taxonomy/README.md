# Drift Taxonomy

A structured visual taxonomy of recurring drift patterns across modern systems.

These are repeatable structural tendencies that emerge when systems remain operational while gradually losing alignment with reality.

Each drift type captures a distinct mechanism. The collection forms a practical taxonomy for recognizing how systems change without visibly breaking.

---

## Drift Types

### Drift Type #01 — Bureaucratic Drift

[View Type](./drift-type-01-bureaucratic-drift.png)

The expansion of procedural layers beyond practical necessity.

Rules multiply.  
Steps accumulate.  
The process becomes heavier than the task.

---

### Drift Type #02 — Incentive Drift

[View Type](./drift-type-02-incentive-drift.png)

When reward structures pull behavior away from actual outcomes.

Metrics begin steering the system more than reality itself.

---

### Drift Type #03 — Institutional Drift

[View Type](./drift-type-03-institutional-drift.png)

Organizations preserving internal coherence while losing external relevance.

The institution remains stable.  
Its relation to the world weakens.

---

### Drift Type #04 — Temporal Drift

[View Type](./drift-type-04-temporal-drift.png)

Breakdown of shared timing, pacing, and temporal coordination.

Everything accelerates, fragments, or delays unevenly.

---

### Drift Type #05 — Customer Service Drift

[View Type](./drift-type-05-customer-service-drift.png)

Support systems increasingly designed to manage volume rather than resolve problems.

Contact becomes procedural rather than relational.

---

## Structural Role

Within the Reality Drift framework:

- **Taxonomy** = how drift is classified  
- **Questions** = where drift becomes conscious  
- **Objects** = what drift looks like  
- **Rituals** = what drift feels like  
- **Archetypes** = who enacts drift  
- **System Language** = how drift legitimizes itself  

Taxonomy matters because recognition becomes sharper when patterns are named.

Without taxonomy, everything feels like isolated frustration. With taxonomy, structure becomes visible.
