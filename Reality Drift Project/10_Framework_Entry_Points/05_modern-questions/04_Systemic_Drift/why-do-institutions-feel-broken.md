# Why Do Institutions Feel Broken?

Part of the Reality Drift framework (2023–2026) by A. Jacobs.

## The Experience

Many people feel that major institutions no longer function the way they once did.

Government agencies appear slow or ineffective. Universities struggle to explain their value. Healthcare systems feel complex and difficult to navigate. Corporations seem highly active but often disconnected from the needs they were originally created to serve.

In many cases these institutions have not collapsed. They still operate, process information, and produce outcomes.

Yet people often experience a growing sense that something fundamental has changed.

This leads to a common question: **why do institutions feel broken even when they are still functioning?**

## What Is Happening

Institutions are designed to absorb complexity from the environment and translate it into stable rules, expectations, and services.

They do this through structures such as professional standards, decision procedures, and shared norms. These structures act as constraints that help organizations maintain alignment with the real conditions they are meant to manage.

Over time, however, institutions can accumulate layers of optimization, administration, and mediation.

New metrics are introduced. Reporting systems expand. Decision processes become more procedural. Communication increasingly occurs through digital systems rather than direct feedback.

As these layers grow, institutions may continue operating internally while becoming less responsive to the conditions outside them.

Within the Reality Drift framework, this pattern is called Constraint Collapse.

Constraint Collapse occurs when the stabilizing structures that once kept a system aligned with real-world conditions weaken or disappear.

## Connection to Reality Drift

The widespread feeling that institutions are failing is often an example of Reality Drift.

Reality Drift occurs when systems remain operational while gradually losing alignment with the realities they were designed to represent.

Several dynamics contribute to institutional drift.

**Constraint Collapse**  
The stabilizing rules, norms, and feedback mechanisms that once guided institutional behavior weaken over time.

**Optimization Trap**  
Institutions increasingly optimize for measurable indicators such as efficiency metrics, compliance targets, or reporting outputs.

**Filter Fatigue**  
Individuals interacting with institutions must interpret increasingly complex systems of information, rules, and signals.

Together these forces can create organizations that remain active and technically functional while feeling disconnected from their original purposes.

## Simple Mechanism

Institutional Complexity  
↓  
Expansion of Metrics and Procedures  
↓  
Weakening of Real-World Feedback  
↓  
Constraint Collapse
