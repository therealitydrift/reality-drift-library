# Incentive Drift Archive (2023–2025)

README v2

A collection of early essays, conceptual notes, and structural observations written under the *Incentive Drift* label prior to the formal development of the Reality Drift framework.

These writings capture recurring concerns surrounding:

- algorithmic systems
- institutional incentives
- media degradation
- consumer behavior
- online identity formation
- economic opacity
- information overload
- cultural fragmentation
- meaning collapse
- synthetic social environments

At the time, these essays were not organized as a unified theoretical system. Most were written as standalone cultural or economic observations reacting to visible changes in technology, media, institutions, and everyday life.

Over time, recurring structural patterns began to emerge across the archive:

- systems preserving appearance while underlying function drifted
- optimization replacing grounding
- incentives reshaping perception
- representation replacing direct contact
- institutions remaining operational while trust weakened
- abundance producing fragmentation rather than stability

These recurring dynamics later stabilized into the broader Reality Drift framework.
