## The Age of Drift: Why Modern Life Feels Fake

*Originally published December 8, 2025*

Over the past few years, I’ve been tracing a structural pattern underlying much of the psychological disorientation of modern life.

Meaning degrades under sustained overload, compression, and misalignment.

*The Age of Drift* is a compiled set of research notes, conceptual essays, and early models developed during the initial formation of the Reality Drift framework. These writings examine how modern conditions reshape attention, identity, and meaning. As information accelerates beyond our capacity to integrate it, recognizable shifts begin to appear:

fatigue that doesn’t quite resolve

a growing need to filter and narrow attention

subtle shifts in identity and self-perception

difficulty anchoring to time or continuity

a sense of slight distance from one’s own experience

the impression that everything moves quickly, yet little feels settled

The essays trace how drift begins, how sensemaking systems strain under sustained optimization pressure, and how digital environments intensify those effects. They capture the early development of ideas that would later become central to the framework, including synthetic realness, the optimization trap, and the drift principle.
