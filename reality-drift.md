# Reality Drift

Part of the Reality Drift framework (2023–2026) by A. Jacobs

## Canonical Definition

Reality Drift is a systems-level condition in which institutions, technologies, and cultural environments continue to function while gradually losing alignment with real-world feedback and lived experience.

It emerges when optimization pressures and mediation layers intensify, preserving coherence and performance metrics while weakening contextual grounding.

As efficiency and abstraction increase, alignment erodes and meaning becomes unstable over time. 

## What Makes It Distinct

Reality Drift builds on earlier analyses of alienation, hyperreality, and media mediation.

Those frameworks often describe distortion, simulation, or dislocation as cultural or philosophical conditions.

Reality Drift instead describes an operational pattern in which systems continue to function and perform while gradually diverging from the real-world conditions they were designed to reflect.

## Mechanism

Modern systems optimize for measurable indicators such as:

- engagement
- efficiency
- compliance
- growth

Paradoxically, this optimization increases divergence from underlying purpose.

Common dynamics include:

- Metrics substituting for meaning  
- Representation replacing direct feedback  
- Processes continuing even as grounding weakens

## How It Shows Up

Reality Drift is recognizable across modern life:

- Political discourse feels scripted regardless of substance  
- Cultural products feel iterative rather than original  
- News cycles repeat patterns until events blur together  
- Organizational dashboards simulate productivity while obscuring value  
- Individuals adapt behavior to algorithmic incentives rather than lived priorities

## Cross-Domain Effects

Reality Drift extends across multiple domains:

**Psychology / Neuroscience**  
Individuals experience disorientation when optimized signals no longer align with lived context.

**AI / Technology**  
Systems produce fluent outputs that remain internally coherent while drifting from user intent or real-world grounding.

**Education**  
Learning environments prioritize measurable outputs over deep understanding.

**Work / Organizations**  
Performance indicators substitute for value creation.

**Culture / Media**  
Engagement optimization amplifies performance and narrative over reference and substance.

## In One Sentence

Reality Drift is the systemic misalignment that occurs when optimization preserves coherence while eroding the contextual grounding that sustains meaning.
